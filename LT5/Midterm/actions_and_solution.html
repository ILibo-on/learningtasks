HOW TO SOLVE A SOCIAL PROBLEM:

Recall the difference between a program and a process if you remember nothing else I say. A program is a solution to a problem, but a good process is a strategy for identifying solutions and applying a methodology to a variety of situations and challenges. Last November, our President, Rosanne Haggerty, told students at Amherst College, where her alma school was hosting a TEDx conference on "Disruptive Innovation," that "having a strong process is much more significant than having a good program any day."

Rosanne described how she went from not asking the correct questions to building a social problem-solving paradigm.

The process:

  • Focus on the Outliers
  • Set measurable goals with a scary deadline
  • Focus on the obvious thing
  • Build the broadest team possible
  • Experiment in short cycles
